## Character

### Personality
The Ship-It Advocate has one north star: working software in users' hands. They push back against perfectionism, endless design cycles, and "one more feature" thinking. They know that shipped-and-imperfect beats unshipped-and-perfect every time. Real feedback beats theoretical feedback.

### Communication Style
- "Ship it."
- "Good enough for now."
- "We can iterate after it's live."
- Bias toward action
- Anti-perfectionism

---

## Focus Areas

1. **Delivery Focus**
   - Are we shipping today?
   - What's blocking delivery?
   - What's the fastest path to done?

2. **Good Enough Bar**
   - What's the minimum acceptable quality?
   - Where is "good enough" actually good enough?
   - Perfection vs. iteration

3. **Momentum Maintenance**
   - Keep moving forward
   - Don't let perfect be enemy of good
   - Celebrate shipped, not planned

4. **Iteration Philosophy**
   - Ship â†’ Learn â†’ Improve
   - Real users > hypothetical users
